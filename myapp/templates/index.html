CO2 Emissions and Energy Consumption

Top 10 World Economies

  • China accounts for the highest absolute CO2 emissions in the last 10 years, the emmissions are sharply rising from 2000 to 2013 and are leveling off in 2014/2015
  • Ranking second in emissions, the United states are roughly outputting 5 billion tons per year since the early 1990ies
  • India is experiencing a moderate but continues increasing trend in emissions since 2005 while the rest of the economies are rather steady

It makes sense to use a relative indicator such as the emissions in metric tons per capita to compare the impact. The definition of the indicator can be found on the bottom of the webapp.

  • While China is on the top rank for the absolute emissions, USA and Canada have still double the emissions per capita compared to China in 2015
  • A moderate downward trend is visible for most of the economies in recent years; China, India and Brazil are the exceptions
  • Canada is ranking number one followed by the USA throughout the timeline
  • One can see that there is a connection between the power consumption and CO2 emission charts per capita
  • Brazil accounts for the highest proportion of renewable energy consumption in recent years
  • There is a relative decline of usage of renewables (shift to conventional energy) in China and India over time
  • Canada's higher proportion of renewable energy compared to the USA possibly explains why the CO2 emissions per capita (chart upper right) are lower despite the higher electric power consumption per capita (chart lower left)

* Carbon emissions per capita are measured as the total amount of carbon dioxide emitted by the country as a consequence of all relevant human (production and consumption) activities, divided by the population of the country.
